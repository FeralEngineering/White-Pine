# White Pine

*An intentionally impractical device for tracking white pine sightings and cultivating attentiveness to the natural world.*

## Design Philosophy

Most modern technology is designed to keep us looking at it.

White Pine was designed to do the opposite.

Its purpose is not to pull the user into a digital environment, but to encourage engagement with the physical one. Each button press represents a moment of recognition: a white pine noticed along a trail, beside a road, or growing quietly in a familiar place.

At first glance, White Pine appears to be a counter for white pine sightings. In practice, it's a counter for moments when the user noticed one.

The project is intentionally narrow in scope. It does not need cloud synchronization, analytics, dashboards, gamification, species databases, productivity metrics, social features, or a mobile app. Those additions would make it more complicated and less meaningful.

White Pine is built around the idea that a simple object can encourage a different way of moving through the world.

---

## What Is It?

White Pine is a handheld electronic device dedicated to a single species: the Eastern White Pine (*Pinus strobus*).

Each time a white pine is spotted, a button press records the sighting and updates the device's count.

That's all it does.

The hardware exists in service of the idea, not the other way around.

---

## Features

- Lifetime white pine sighting counter
- Current session counter
- Rechargeable battery
- Pocket-sized handheld form factor
- Built from salvaged and repurposed components
- Designed for simplicity and long-term use

---

## Design Decisions

### The Screen Remains Off

A sighting is recorded with a button press, but the display does not illuminate.

This is intentional.

The device is meant to keep attention on the landscape rather than on itself. Immediate visual feedback would shift focus from the observation to the device.

The display is only shown when explicitly requested through a long button press.

### No Reset Button

The lifetime count is intentionally permanent.

Observations made during testing, assembly, debugging, and everyday use become part of the device's history.

The count is not intended to be pristine. It is intended to be honest.

### No Historical Log

White Pine records only that a sighting occurred.

It does not record when, where, or under what circumstances.

The observation belongs to the person who experienced it, not the device.

### No Additional Features

Future development should focus on refinement rather than expansion.

Potential improvements may include:

- Improved enclosure design
- Better power efficiency
- Display and interface refinements
- More integrated hardware

If White Pine ever gained cloud sync, analytics, dashboards, species databases, achievement systems, or similar features, it would have lost sight of what made the project worth building in the first place.

---

## Build Notes

The current prototype was built using salvaged and repurposed components, including a recovered lithium battery, and evolved through multiple design iterations focused on portability, durability, and battery life.

Like the device itself, the project embraces reuse, experimentation, and making tangible things from materials already at hand.

---

## Why White Pine?

The original idea was simply to build a counter dedicated to a single tree species for the goofy simplicity of it.

As the project developed, the interesting part stopped being the counter itself and became the behavior it encouraged.

The device only works if the user is paying attention to their surroundings. A white pine can be standing twenty feet away and the counter will never change unless it is actually noticed.

That realization ended up informing most of the design decisions. The screen stays off during normal use. There is no historical log. There are no maps, notifications, achievements, or statistics.

The goal was never to collect data.

The goal was to create a small object that encourages its user to look up.
